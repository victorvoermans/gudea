Last updated: 2024/10/21 (Cylinder A, column 1).

Gudea was the ruler of the Sumerian city-state of Lagas. The city god of Lagas was Ninƒùirsu, and his house (there is no separate Sumerian word for ‘temple’, a temple is the house of a god) was called the Eninnu. This text, which was written around 2100 BC and spans two large cylinders, is about Gudea’s rebuilding of that temple, told in mythological terms. Enlil, whose name appears early on in the text, was the chief god of the Sumerian pantheon.
